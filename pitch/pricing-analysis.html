When to use which model — segment guide
DEO Members — Why discounted AND free tier
DEO pricing is exclusive to members. The free tier ($60/referral vs $85 standard) is the DEO Cost Savings Club value prop — concrete savings they can see on day one, zero commitment. The monthly/annual discounts reward members who commit. Non-DEO practices don't get the free tier or the monthly discounts.

This makes DEO membership more valuable: "Sign up for DEO, get Rippl free to start at a rate 30% below what everyone else pays."
DEO monthly discount: ~33% off standard
Standard (Non-DEO) — No free tier, higher per-referral rate
Non-DEO practices can use Rippl at standard pricing. No free tier — they must start on a monthly plan. If you choose to offer a pay-per-referral entry to standard customers, it's $85/referral (vs $60 DEO) — the gap creates a concrete DEO membership benefit. Standard monthly rates ($599, $999, etc.) are the non-discounted rack rates.
Standard rack rate: no DEO discount applied
Automotive (CarLock model) — Hybrid always
Automotive uses a hybrid model by design: $299/mo platform + $50/closed referral deal. This is because automotive referrals are higher-value (avg $3K-5K gross profit per deal) and lower-frequency than dental. The base fee covers the SFTP integration cost. The per-referral fee scales with their success. No free tier for automotive — the integration cost alone justifies the base fee.
Always hybrid: base + per-referral
The Hybrid Model ($199 + $40/ref DEO) — The Revenue-Maximizing Middle
A hybrid option gives David a revenue floor (the base fee) plus upside as referral volume grows. At almost every referral volume above 5/month, hybrid beats flat monthly for David. Consider offering this as an option alongside flat monthly — let the practice choose. High-referral practices will self-select flat monthly; low-referral practices will self-select hybrid or free.
Hybrid crossover vs flat: ~8 referrals/mo
